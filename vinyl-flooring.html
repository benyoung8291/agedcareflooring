Why Vinyl Works in Aged Care

01

Infection Control

Heat-welded seams mean no grout lines for bacteria to hide in. That's the whole point. Seamless installation eliminates the harbourage points that traditional tiling can't avoid. Your cleaning staff doesn't need to dig into corners with a toothbrush. Standards compliance — NSQHS and Aged Care Quality Standards — just works because the design is right.

02

Slip Resistance

We specify vinyl ranges rated R10-R12 — measured to AS 4586:2013 and verified in-situ after installation. The important bit: that grip holds even when wet, even after spills, even in bathrooms where you can't control moisture. Your residents and staff get stability on every floor, every shift.

03

Actually Low Maintenance

No waxing rituals. No sealing schedules. No stripping every two years. Standard commercial cleaners, mop, done. Seamless floors clean faster because there are no tile seams trapping dirt. One-piece sheets mean the floor stays flat, stays stable, and doesn't separate at seams after a decade.

04

Mobility Matters

Seamless surfaces don't snag wheelchair wheels or catch walker feet. Heat-welded seams create one continuous surface — no tile edges or raised joints. Residents can move independently between spaces without negotiating hazards that shouldn't exist. That matters for dignity and safety.

How We Specify & Install Safety Vinyl

Installation detail matters. Cut corners here and you'll regret it in five years when seams separate, water seeps underneath, or the floor starts moving. We do it right.

Heat-Welded Seams, Not Glued Edges

Seamless vinyl isn't seamless if the seams fail. We heat-weld every joint — the thermoplastic gets melted together, creating one continuous surface. That's different from taping or gluing. Glued seams separate after a few years of floor traffic and cleaning. Heat-welded seams last decades and maintain watertight integrity through it all.

  • Seams stay intact through years of cleaning and disinfection
  • Water doesn't seep underneath and cause subfloor damage
  • No bacteria-trapping gaps at joints
  • Zero maintenance on seams — they outlast the floor

Coved Skirting in Wet Areas

That junction where the floor meets the wall? Traditional straight corners trap moisture and bacteria. Coved skirting curves up and eliminates the trap. It's a 90-degree problem solved with curved vinyl. Your cleaning staff actually appreciates it — no more scrubbing into impossible corners. NSQHS standards expect this in clinical spaces.

  • Moisture and bacteria don't accumulate in floor-wall corners
  • Faster, easier cleaning with fewer corners to get into
  • Professional appearance that passes compliance inspections
  • Standard in hospital and healthcare specifications worldwide

Proper Subfloor Preparation

We don't just lay vinyl on top of whatever's there. We test for flatness, moisture, and stability. We repair imperfections, apply acoustic or moisture underlayment as needed, and select adhesives that work with your specific subfloor. Rushing this part creates movement, noise, wear, and eventual seam separation. None of that is acceptable in aged care.

  • Flatness testing ensures proper adhesion and no foot traffic hot spots
  • Moisture testing prevents subfloor rot and floor movement
  • Acoustic underlayment reduces noise in multi-story facilities
  • Right adhesive + right subfloor = floor that stays flat for 20 years

Installation That Doesn't Shut Down Your Facility

Your facility can't close for floor renovations. We schedule installation after hours, stage work by zone, and coordinate with your care teams. Subfloor prep happens in advance so installation runs fast. Your residents stay comfortable. Staff keeps working. The facility keeps operating. That's how it should work.
